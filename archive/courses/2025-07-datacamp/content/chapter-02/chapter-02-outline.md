## Lesson 2.1: "From Idea to Strategic Prompt"

**Target:** 450-500 words, ~4.5-5 minutes  
**Objectives:** Break down complex ideas systematically, craft effective strategic prompts

### Script Structure

**Opening Hook (30s)**
- Bridge from Chapter 1: "You've got the mindset and framework"
- Challenge: Real app = more complexity than timer
- Promise: Systematic breakdown + strategic prompting makes anything buildable

**The Project (45s)**
- What we're building: Lead submission form with custom kanban backend
- Why this matters: Real business value, sales pipeline management
- Not just a tutorial project—something you'd actually use

**Systems Thinking (90s)**

**User Stories First (30s):** Who needs what?
- Potential customers: Submit lead information easily
- Sales team: Manage leads, track pipeline progression
- System: Secure, scalable, maintainable

**Component Mapping (30s):** Translate needs to features
- Lead capture → Dynamic lead submission form
- Lead management → Custom kanban backend with pipeline stages
- User experience → Clean interface + mobile-friendly

**Technical Reality Check (30s):** What AI handles vs. what you decide
- AI: Database structure, authentication setup, styling details
- You: User experience decisions, business logic, workflow design

**Strategic Prompt Construction (90s)**

**Context Setting (30s):** Descriptive, not prescriptive
- Give AI the vision and constraints
- Leave room for AI to surprise with better solutions
- Focus on getting the high-level architecture right

**Breaking Down Our App (30s):** What are we actually building?
- Visual mapping helps: sketch it out first
- Two main components: Lead submission UI + Admin dashboard
- Design feel: Lightweight and minimalist
- Decide what to build vs. what not to build (scope control)

**User Flow Visualization (30s):** Connect the dots
- Lead submission → Database storage → Admin dashboard view
- Clear data flow prevents feature creep
- Simple path = better user experience

**Prompt Engineering Principles (60s)**
- **Descriptive, not prescriptive:** Explain the outcome, not the implementation
- **Design inspiration:** Reference visual styles and user experience patterns
- **Smart constraints:** Guide without limiting AI creativity
- **Context over commands:** Set the stage for intelligent decision-making

**What We Deliberately Exclude (30s)**
- User authentication (future iteration)
- Advanced kanban functionality (start simple)
- Complex business logic (focus on core workflow)

**Bridge (15s)**
- "Now let's understand what to expect when we send this"

### Slides
1. Hook: "Real apps need systematic approach"
2. Project preview: Lead submission form system
3. User stories: Who needs what?
4. Component mapping: Needs to features
5. AI handles vs. you decide
6. Context first: Setting the stage
7. Outcome focus: Success criteria
8. Smart constraints: Guide without limiting
9. Complete prompt: Ready for execution
10. Transition: "What to expect from AI"

## Lesson 2.2: "Understanding AI Outcomes and Execution"

**Target:** 450-500 words, ~4.5-5 minutes  
**Objectives:** Understand AI variability, execute first prompt, assess and iterate on results

### Script Structure

**Opening (20s)**
- Bridge: "We've crafted our strategic prompt"
- This lesson: Understand what to expect, then execute

**Understanding AI Outcomes (120s)**

**Likelihood and Distribution (40s):** What to expect
- AI responses exist on a spectrum of possibilities
- Same prompt = different but valid approaches
- Your prompt influences probability, doesn't guarantee specifics
- Example: 5 developers, same requirements, 5 different valid solutions

**Embracing Variability (40s):** Why this is actually good
- Multiple valid solutions to any problem
- AI might surprise you with better approaches
- Innovation happens in the unexpected
- Focus on whether it solves your core need

**Setting Expectations (40s):** Success metrics
- Does it fulfill the user stories we defined?
- Is the core functionality working?
- Can we build on this foundation?
- Perfect is the enemy of shipped

**Execution in Replit (180s)**
- **Setup Replit (30s):** New project, environment ready
- **Sending our prompt (30s):** The moment of truth
- **Watching AI work (60s):** Real-time generation and explanation
- **First assessment (60s):** What we got vs. what we planned

**Testing and Initial Iteration (90s)**
- **Core functionality test (30s):** Does it work as intended?
- **User experience check (30s):** Does it meet our user stories?
- **Quick iteration (30s):** One focused improvement

**Foundation Analysis (45s)**
- What's solid to build on
- What exceeded expectations
- What needs attention in advanced features
- The power of starting with working code

**Key Insights (25s)**
- AI variability is a feature, not a bug
- First execution teaches you about both AI and your problem
- Working foundation beats perfect planning
- Every response is a collaboration, not a command

**Wrap (20s)**
- What we accomplished: Live system from strategic thinking
- Next chapter: Advanced features and refinement

### Slides
1. "Ready to execute our strategic prompt"
2. AI outcomes: Likelihood and distribution spectrum
3. Embracing variability: Innovation in the unexpected
4. Setting expectations: Success metrics
5. Replit setup: Execution environment
6. Sending our prompt: The moment of truth
7. Watching AI work: Real-time generation
8. First assessment: What we got vs. planned
9. Testing: Core functionality and user experience
10. Foundation analysis: Building on solid ground
11. Key insights: Variability as feature
