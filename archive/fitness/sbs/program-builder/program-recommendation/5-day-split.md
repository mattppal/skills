<recovery_management>
## Comprehensive Recovery Framework

### Inter-Session Recovery Requirements
```
Movement Pattern Separation:
- Minimum 24h between upper/lower body sessions
- 48h between similar movement patterns
- 72h between high-intensity compound sessions

Recovery Quality Indicators:
- Sleep quality and duration (7-9 hours optimal)
- Subjective energy levels upon waking
- Motivation for training session
- Absence of persistent muscle soreness
```

### Weekly Recovery Structure
```
Training Pattern: 5 consecutive days, 2 rest days
Recovery Strategies:
- Active recovery on rest days (light mobility, walking)
- Contrast showers or sauna post-training
- Adequate protein intake (1.6-2.2g/kg bodyweight)
- Strategic carbohydrate timing around training
```

### Deload Implementation
```
Frequency: Every 4-6 weeks or when performance indicators decline

Deload Options:
1. Volume Deload (Recommended):
   - Reduce working sets by 50%
   - Maintain intensity at 80-85% of working loads
   - Keep movement patterns and exercise selection

2. Intensity Deload:
   - Reduce RPE targets by 2 points
   - Maintain volume and exercise selection
   - Focus on movement quality and technique refinement

3. Complete Active Rest:
   - 5-7 days of light activity only
   - Mobility work and general physical preparation
   - Reserved for extreme fatigue situations
```
</recovery_management>

<progression_framework>
## Systematic Progressive Overload

### Primary Movement Progression
```
Weekly Assessment Protocol:
1. Evaluate RPE accuracy on primary lifts
2. Assess total reps completed across working sets
3. Monitor bar speed and technique quality

Progression Criteria:
- RPE < 8: Increase load by 2.5-5lbs next week
- RPE = 8: Maintain current load
- RPE > 8: Reduce load by 2.5lbs or maintain

Load Increment Guidelines:
- Squat/Deadlift: 2.5-5kg (5-10lbs)
- Bench Press: 1.25-2.5kg (2.5-5lbs)
- Overhead Press: 1.25kg (2.5lbs)
```

### Secondary and Accessory Progression
```
Bi-Weekly Progression Assessment:
- Progress when hitting upper rep ranges consistently
- Focus on quality of execution over load increases
- Rotate exercises every 4-6 weeks to prevent staleness

Progression Hierarchy:
1. Increase reps within prescribed range
2. Add additional set when rep targets achieved
3. Increase load when volume targets met
4. Introduce movement variations for continued stimulus
```
</progression_framework>

## Advanced Implementation Guidelines

### Exercise Selection Principles
```
Primary Movement Rotation:
- Maintain consistency for 8-12 weeks minimum
- Consider minor variations every 3-4 weeks
- Base changes on weakness identification and goals

Secondary Movement Rotation:
- Change every 4-6 weeks based on adaptation
- Select based on primary lift support needs

Accessory Rotation:
- Change every 3-4 weeks to prevent staleness

```
Session Monitoring:
- Track training duration and subjective session RPE
- Monitor primary lift performance trends
- Assess sleep quality and recovery metrics

Weekly Evaluation:
- Calculate average session completion rates
- Review strength progression across primary lifts
- Evaluate overall training satisfaction and sustainability

Monthly Assessment:
- Body composition and performance testing
- Program adherence and satisfaction evaluation
- Adjustment of volume and intensity based on response
```

```
Block Periodization:
- 4-week strength focus blocks
