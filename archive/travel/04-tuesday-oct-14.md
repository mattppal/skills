## WHAT TO PACK FOR BOAT

### Must Bring
- Swimsuit (wear it)
- Towel (they may provide but bring your own to be safe)
- Reef-safe sunscreen (reapply before getting in water)
- Light jacket or long sleeve (boat ride can be windy/cool)
- Hat, sunglasses
- Dramamine if prone to seasickness (take 30-60 mins before departure)

### Optional
- Underwater camera or GoPro (many tours rent GoPros for $30-40)
- Prescription swim goggles if you can't wear contacts with mask
- Wetsuit if you get cold (boats usually provide)

### Leave Behind
- Valuables (leave at accommodation)
- Large bags (minimal storage on boat)
- Regular sunscreen (not reef-safe = not allowed)

### Bring Cash For
- Crew tips ($10-20 per person)
- GoPro rental if you want photos
- Any snacks/drinks at harbor

## WHAT TO EXPECT ON BOAT

### Boat Ride
- Can be choppy depending on conditions
- Sit outside if prone to seasickness
- 30-40 min ride each way
- Crew will give safety briefing

### Snorkeling
- Water temp: ~75-78°F (comfortable)
- Visibility: Usually 100+ feet (incredible)
- Depth: You stay at surface, water below can be 20-80 feet
- Current: Usually minimal inside crater
- Life jacket available if you want one

### What You'll See
- Molokini: Tropical fish, coral, possibly turtles, clear blue water
- Second stop: High chance of sea turtles, more coral
- Dolphins sometimes
- The water clarity is unreal

### Food Included
- Breakfast: Usually continental (muffins, fruit, coffee)
- Lunch: Varies by tour (sandwiches, wraps, snacks)
- Drinks: Water, juice, sometimes soda
