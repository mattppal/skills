### TOP HIKES (2-3 Hour Range)

**1. Waimoku Falls via Pipiwai Trail - #1 Rated**
- **Distance:** 3.4 miles round trip
- **Time:** 2-2.5 hours
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Rating:** 4.8/5 (9,214 reviews)
- **AllTrails:** https://www.alltrails.com/trail/hawaii/maui/pipiwai-trail
- **Why it's special:** This unique waterfall hike in Haleakalā National Park takes you through a stunning bamboo forest before reaching the falls. You'll pass several great waterfalls on the way. Note: This requires driving the famous Road to Hana (60 miles, 620 curves, 59 bridges) - make this a full day adventure!
- **Pro tip:** Start early to beat crowds and enjoy the mystical bamboo forest.

**2. Seven Sacred Pools Trail ('Ohe'o Gulch)**
- **Distance:** 0.6 miles
- **Time:** 0.5-1 hour
- **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Rating:** 4.6/5 (1,795 reviews)
- **Why it's special:** Beautiful ocean views, lush vegetation, pools, and a waterfall. Located at Kipahulu Visitor Center. Can be combined with Pipiwai Trail for a full day.

**3. Waihee Ridge Trail**
- **Distance:** 4.2 miles round trip
- **Time:** 2.5-3 hours
- **Difficulty:** Moderate-Hard (1,500 ft elevation gain)
- **AllTrails:** https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/hawaii/waihee-ridge-trail
- **Why it's special:** TOP 5 scenic hike on Maui. Panoramic valley and ocean views from ridge, lush forests, less touristy. Consistently rated as one of the best VIEW hikes on the island.

**4. Sliding Sands Trail (Haleakala Crater)**
- **Distance:** 2-11 miles (out and back as far as you want)
- **Time:** 1-5 hours depending how far you go
- **Difficulty:** Moderate (downhill going in, uphill coming out at 10,000 ft elevation)
- **Rating:** 4.8/5 (772 reviews)
- **AllTrails:** https://www.alltrails.com/trail/hawaii/maui/sliding-sands-keonehe-ehe-e-trail
- **Why it's special:** Descends into Haleakala crater with otherworldly volcanic landscape. Extraterrestrial scenery, unique silverswords. Can turn around at any point.

**5. Halemau'u Trail (Haleakala)**
- **Distance:** 3-7 miles round trip
- **Time:** 2-4 hours
- **Difficulty:** Moderate to Hard
- **Rating:** 4.7/5 (1,596 reviews)
- **AllTrails:** https://www.alltrails.com/trail/hawaii/maui/halemauu-haleakala-overlook-trail
- **Why it's special:** Descends into crater with greener landscapes, wildflowers, different perspective than Sliding Sands.

## PRO TIPS FOR YOUR ADVENTURE

**Beach Exploration:**
1. **Best beach times:** Early morning (7-9am) for calm water and fewer people, or late afternoon (4-6pm) for sunset
2. **Tide pools:** Check tide schedules - low tide is best for exploring tide pools at Keawakapu Beach
3. **Hidden beaches:** Ask locals at Kahale's for their favorite secret spots
4. **Beach gear:** Bring snorkel gear, reef-safe sunscreen, water shoes for rocky areas

**Molokini Snorkeling:**
5. **Book early morning tours** (6-7am departure) - calmest water, best visibility, fewer people.
6. **Prone to seasickness?** Take Dramamine 30-60 min before departure. Sit outside on the boat.
7. **Photography:** Most tours offer GoPro rentals ($30-40). Capture those turtle moments!

**Road to Hana:**
8. **5am departure is NON-NEGOTIABLE** - You want 2-3 hours before crowds hit Pipiwai Trail.
9. **Pack:** Breakfast, snacks, 2-3 liters water per person, downloaded maps (service is spotty).
10. **Gas up night before** - Fill tank in Kihei. Limited stations on route.
11. **Bathroom strategy:** Go at every opportunity. Pipiwai trailhead has facilities.
12. **Swimsuit under clothes** - You'll want to jump in pools/waterfalls.

**Haleakala Sunset:**
13. **No reservation needed** - Unlike sunrise, sunset doesn't require recreation.gov booking
14. **Best of both worlds** - Beach/relax in morning, adventure in afternoon, no 3am wake-up
15. **Dress in LAYERS** - Summit is 30-40°F even at sunset. Bring winter jacket, pants, blanket, beanie
16. **Bring headlamp/flashlight** - For drive down after dark
17. **October sunset time** - Around 6:15-6:30pm, check exact time day-of
18. **Less crowded** - Way fewer people than sunrise, more peaceful experience

**Meeting Locals:**
19. **Kahale's dive bar** - Best spot. Arrive 7-8pm, live music starts ~8pm. Just be friendly and chat at the bar.
20. **Ask for local recommendations** - Tell people it's your first time. Locals LOVE to share secret spots.

**Coffee & Writing:**
21. **Akamai has no wifi** (by design) - Bring phone hotspot or just embrace focused writing.
22. **Best writing times:** Early morning (6-8am) or mid-afternoon (2-4pm) between adventures.

**Logistics:**
23. **Aloha Boards closed Sundays** - Hit them Monday-Saturday for authentic skate/surf gear.
24. **Reef-safe sunscreen ONLY** - Hawaii law. Available at all surf shops. Regular sunscreen kills coral.
25. **Early mornings are KEY** - Most magic happens before 10am (beaches, snorkel, hikes, waterfalls).
26. **Gym strategy:** Maui Powerhouse on late afternoons (Sat, Mon, Tues) and Thursday morning before departure.

**Food & Drinks:**
27. **Kihei Caffe** - Legendary local breakfast spot. Go early (7-8am) or expect 30-45 min wait.
28. **Happy hour** - Many restaurants have 3-5pm happy hour. Great for budget-conscious eating.
29. **Food trucks** - Some of the best food on island. Kraken Coffee location has multiple trucks.

**Weather & Ocean:**
30. **October weather:** Mid-80s°F days, high-60s nights. Occasional morning showers. Perfect!
31. **Ocean safety:** If you're not sure about conditions, ask locals or lifeguards. Respect the ocean.
32. **Waves pick up in afternoon** - Morning swims are calmer, afternoon is better for beach lounging/reading.
33. **Beach variety:** Big Beach for expansive views, Secret Cove for seclusion, Kamaole for facilities/convenience.
