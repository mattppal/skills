### AFTERNOON - HALEAKALA NATIONAL PARK

**3:30pm - Arrive at Summit Area**
- Stop at Park Visitor Center
- Pay entrance fee if needed (~$30 per vehicle, good for 3 days)
- Get trail map
- Check sunset time
- Use facilities

**4:00pm - HIKE SLIDING SANDS TRAIL INTO CRATER**
- **Trailhead:** Near summit visitor center (can't miss it)
- **Find on AllTrails:** Search "Sliding Sands Haleakala" (you'll see 18-mile option - ignore that!)
- **Official NPS:** https://www.nps.gov/hale/planyourvisit/hiking.htm

**IMPORTANT - How Far You're Actually Hiking:**
- **NOT the full 18-mile trail** (that's for backpackers going to remote cabins)
- **You're doing:** 2-4 miles TOTAL (out and back)
- **Time before sunset:** 2-2.5 hours max

**Realistic Options:**
- **1 mile in, 1 mile back = 2 miles total:** 45 mins-1 hour (good views)
- **1.5 miles in, 1.5 miles back = 3 miles total:** 1.5 hours (great crater views)
- **2 miles in, 2 miles back = 4 miles total:** 2 hours (deep into crater, stunning)

**DO NOT try to hike the full 18-mile trail!** That's an all-day or multi-day backpacking route to Paliku Cabin. You're just going into the crater until you get good views, then turning around for sunset.
  
**What You'll Experience:**
- Descend into volcanic crater
- Otherworldly landscape (feels like Mars)
- Colorful volcanic cinder
- Silversword plants (unique to Haleakala)
- Golden hour lighting
- Getting quieter as sunset approaches

**Trail Notes:**
- Downhill going IN (easy)
- UPHILL coming OUT at 10,000 ft elevation (harder!)
- Pace yourself, thin air
- Turn around by 5:45pm to get back for sunset
- Can be windy in crater

**6:00pm - Return to Summit/Crater Rim**
- Find your sunset viewing spot
- Popular spots: Near visitor center, Kalahaku Overlook, crater rim
- Get there 15-20 mins before sunset

**6:15-6:30pm - SUNSET ABOVE THE CLOUDS**
- Watch the sun set from 10,000 feet
- Above the cloud layer
- Colors are insane
- Temperature drops fast after sunset
- This is why you're here
- Take photos, but also just experience it

**6:45pm - Stars Begin to Appear**
- Some of the best stargazing in the world
- Give eyes 10 mins to adjust
- Milky Way visible
- Constellations crystal clear
