**7:15am-10:00am - WAIHEE RIDGE TRAIL HIKE**
- **Distance:** 4.2 miles round trip
- **Time:** 2.5-3 hours (take your time)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate-Hard (1,500 ft elevation gain)
- **Rating:** TOP 5 SCENIC HIKE ON MAUI
- **AllTrails:** https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/hawaii/waihee-ridge-trail

**What You'll Experience:**
- **First section:** Steep ascent through lush forest
- **Mid-hike:** Open ridge with PANORAMIC valley views
- **Summit area:** 360-degree views - Waihee Valley, ocean, mountains
- **Flora:** Native Hawaiian plants, lush vegetation, possible rain forest canopy
- **Photo ops:** Incredible viewpoints along ridge
- **Why it's special:** One of the best VIEW hikes on Maui, less touristy than Iao

**Trail Notes:**
- Starts steep (warm up those legs!)
- Can be muddy and slippery in sections
- Bring trekking poles if you have them
- Exposed ridge sections (sun protection important)
- Often misty/cloudy (can add to views or obscure them - take what you get)
- Less crowded than tourist hikes
- Serious elevation gain - pace yourself
- Some locals say this is THE best view hike on Maui

**What to Bring:**
- 2+ liters water (you'll need it)
- Snacks/energy bars
- Hiking poles (helpful for steep sections)
- Rain jacket (weather changes fast)
- Camera (the views are incredible)

## SAFETY NOTES

### Ocean Safety
- Never turn your back on the ocean
- Watch for shore break at Big Beach (can be powerful)
- If in doubt, don't go out
- Stay on designated paths at Ahihi-Kinau (lava rock is sharp)

### Sun Protection
- Reapply sunscreen every 2 hours minimum
- Take shade breaks
- Wear hat and sunglasses
- Drink water constantly (it's easy to get dehydrated)

### Wildlife
- Sea turtles: Look but don't touch (must stay 10+ feet away - federal law)
- Fish: Don't feed them
- Coral: Don't step on it or touch it
