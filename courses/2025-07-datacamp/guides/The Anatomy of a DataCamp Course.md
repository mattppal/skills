A DataCamp course is built on a structured foundation to create a consistent, engaging, and effective learning experience. This guide outlines how a course is organized, what’s expected at each level, and where to find resources to help you build outstanding didactic content.

## Course Structure

Every DataCamp course is composed of **2 to 4 chapters**, and each chapter contains **lessons**. A **lesson** is the smallest pedagogical unit and consists of:

  

*   **1 video exercise** (2–4 minutes long)
*   **2 to 3 interactive exercises** (such as multiple choice, coding, or drag-and-drop)

  

This design ensures that learners can absorb new concepts through short, focused videos and then apply them immediately.

## Outlines and Learning Objectives

As part of your work with DataCamp, you will receive a course outline, where we provide a detailed description of each lesson's learning objectives and the type of exercises to create for each lesson. Here is an example outline of the **first chapter** of the Introduction to AI Agents course.

### Chapter 1: Foundations of AI Agents

**Lesson 1.1 – What is an AI Agent?**

**Learning Objectives:** 

*   Understand and define the definition of an AI agent, distinguishing it from related topics such as Generative AI and Workflow Automation. 

**Exercise 1 — Video Exercise:** 

*   Script introducing the course story (Welcome to the course, by the end of this course, you will be able to …)
*   Define what an AI Agent is — Contextualize the definition of an agent with an example ([similar to the HuggingFace course](https://huggingface.co/learn/agents-course/en/unit1/what-are-agents))
*   Discussing how AI Agents differ from other forms of AI (GenAI, Workflow Automation) — Contextualize with examples 

**Exercise 2 — Multiple Choice Exercise**: 

*   Choose the definition of an AI agent from a list of 4 possible answers.

**Exercise 3 — Classification Exercise** 

*   Classify given use-cases and examples as AI Agents or not.

  

**Lesson 1.2 – What Makes an Agent Agentic?**

**Learning Objective**:

*   Identify and describe the core components of AI agents—including [Models, Tools, & Orchestration](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1W8EnoPXRLTQesfjvb-b3Zj-dnBf1f--n/view), and Communication Interface
*   Understand the different components of the orchestration layer, including Memory, Knowledge Base, and More.  
*   Contextualize the above with an example. 

**Exercise 1 — Video Exercise:** 

*   Script introducing the core components of AI agents ([Models, Tools, & Orchestration](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1W8EnoPXRLTQesfjvb-b3Zj-dnBf1f--n/view)) — contextualized with an example (e.g., customer support bot that has access to tools, memory, etc..)
*   Describe how agentic systems can grow in maturity and complexity, and share a simplified version of the [framework used here](https://huggingface.co/docs/smolagents/conceptual_guides/intro_agents)
*   Discuss how throughout the remaining lessons, we’ll deepen our understanding of how components like models, orchestration, and tools work together in agentic systems. 

**Exercise 2 — Classification Exercise:**

*   Match different agent actions to different components

**Exercise 3 — Drag and Drop (Order) Exercise:** 

*   Arrange the different maturity levels of AI agents by agency levels 

  

**Lesson 1.3 – To Agent or Not to Agent?**

**Learning Objective:** 

*   Understand the criteria for when to use agents vs other types of tools 
*   Understand how to scope a business problem through the lens of an AI Agent use case 
*   Understand the tooling ecosystem for agents, and there is a spectrum of agentic tools ranging from off-the-shelf tools to open-source frameworks 

**Exercise 1 — Video Exercise:** 

*   Script introducing the criteria for when to use agents vs other types of tools 
*   Understand how to scope a business problem through the lens of an AI Agent use case 
*   Discuss how in the AI Agents Ecosystem, there is a variety of tools that allow you to use, but also build AI agents 

**Exercise 2 — Drag and Drop (Order) Exercise:** 

*   Drag and drop business problems and see if they are solved with an Agentic Use-Case or not  

**Exercise 3 — Drag and Drop (Classify) Exercise:** 

*   Build or Buy — Drag and drop use-cases matched to whether they need to be built from the ground-up, or whether you can use code assistants 

  

When authoring exercises and content based on the outline, ensure we cover and reinforce learning objectives within a given lesson. Practically speaking, videos (scripts and slides) should cover the learning objectives of the lesson, and exercises should reinforce what was covered in the videos.
