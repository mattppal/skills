# Creating Didactic Experiences

To deliver effective and memorable learning at DataCamp, your course should go beyond simply teaching syntax or concepts—it should guide learners through a structured, low-friction, and motivating learning journey. Below are best practices focusing on two critical pillars of instructional design:

## Embed Storytelling in Every Lesson

Narratives activate emotional engagement, contextualize abstract ideas, and improve retention. When introduced through a relatable or intriguing scenario, learners are more likely to stay focused and internalize concepts.

  

When designing lessons, while videos will cover learning objectives ([see best practices for creating effective videos](https://app.clickup.com/24474497/v/dc/qaww1-42092/qaww1-19352)), we encourage you to the best of your ability to introduce a self-contained narrative in the exercises of a given lesson to contextualize the learning objectives.

  

A good story doesn’t need to be elaborate—it just needs to be relevant, engaging, and grounded in a real-world scenario learners can relate to. Here are some do's and don'ts below:

### Do's

*   **Anchor each lesson’s exercises in a unique scenario.** Give learners a clear, focused context (e.g., analyzing marketing performance, cleaning survey data, identifying fraud in transactions).
*   **Ensure the story aligns directly with the lesson’s learning objectives.** The narrative should motivate the specific skills being taught—not distract from them.
*   **Keep the story self-contained within the lesson.** Assume learners may skip around—avoid referencing characters or scenarios from earlier lessons or chapters.
*   **Vary the contexts throughout the course.** Mix industries, domains, and storylines to keep the experience fresh. Use finance, healthcare, sports, e-commerce, sustainability, etc.

### Dont's

*   **Don’t reuse the same scenario across all lessons.** Repetition can lead to learner disengagement. Each lesson should feel distinct.
*   **Don’t overcomplicate or overdramatize the story.** Avoid sci-fi plots, fantasy metaphors, or unrealistic job settings. Focus on believable, relevant situations.
*   **Don’t introduce characters or jargon that aren’t essential to the learning objective.** Stick to clear and accessible framing—avoid requiring learners to remember a cast of characters or a corporate backstory.
*   **Don’t mismatch the scenario and the task.** For example, don’t say “You're analyzing stock trends” and then ask learners to do string manipulation—it feels disconnected.

## Reduce Cognitive Load

Learners only have so much working memory available. Overwhelming them with too many ideas, steps, or unfamiliar elements at once leads to frustration and drop-off. Here are some best practices to avoid increasing cognitive load:

  

*   When designing scripts and videos, ensure that the script covers the learning objectives shared in the outline
*   When designing exercises, make sure that the exercises only test concepts related to the lesson's video exercise
*   For coding exercises, offer meaningful hints and scaffolding
*   Design helpful hints for exercises ([see our exercise type best practices here](https://app.clickup.com/24474497/v/dc/qaww1-42092/qaww1-19152))
