## Voice Modes

### Formal Mode
**When**: Blog posts, documentation, professional content
**Style**: Complete grammar, proper capitalization, technical precision
**Example**: "Here's how you can build your first AI-powered application using modern development practices."

### Casual Mode
**When**: Twitter, social media, community engagement
**Style**: Lowercase styling, abbreviations, contractions, conversational
**Example**: "just shipped my first ai app with cursor → took 20 mins, no cs degree needed. wild how accessible this is now"

### Authentic Mode
**When**: Personal stories, behind-the-scenes, community building
**Style**: Mix of formal/casual, strategic imperfection, genuine voice
**Example**: "Been coding for years but AI tools still blow my mind. Yesterday I built something that would've taken weeks... in an afternoon."

## Content Pillars

1. **Educational Excellence**: Simplify complex topics with practical tutorials
2. **Actionable Strategies**: Immediate, results-oriented frameworks
3. **Evidence-Based**: Ground content in research and proven principles
4. **Inspirational**: Use real examples to drive progress
5. **Community**: Foster inclusive, collaborative learning

## Quality Standard

All content must be: **True** → **Relevant** → **Interesting** → **Clear**

## Writing Guidelines

### Do
- Show, don't tell—use concrete examples and live demonstrations
- Connect features to benefits, emphasize outcomes
- Include personal anecdotes, honest struggles, self-deprecating humor
- Use direct, conversational language
- Preserve authentic voice over polished corporate messaging

### Avoid
- Generic transitions: "Here's the thing about...", "The truth is...", "At the end of the day..."
- Marketing clichés: "Game-changing", "Revolutionary", "Next-level"
- Corporate polish that loses personal connection
- Overwhelming complexity for beginners
- Excessive jargon without explanation
